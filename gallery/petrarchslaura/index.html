Petrarch's Laura

Friday August 24, 2018

All three of the heroines of Proust’s Duchess claimed descent from famous historical & cultural figures. One of Laure de Chevigné’s favorite ancestors was her 14th-century namesake Laure de Noves, Comtesse Hugues de Sade, better known as Petrarch’s “Laura.” The Italian poet spotted the 17-year-old married chatelaine from afar at Good Friday services in her native Avignon in 1327, then proceeded to write love poem after love poem in her honor. Among the metaphors Petrarch used to describe her, inspired by her blond hair, was that of a bird with golden feathers, as this illustration from his Canzoniere shows. Marcel Proust, who was captivated by Laure de Chevigné’s “Petrarchan” genealogy, would likewise compare her to an exquisite avian creature. (In a less idealizing vein, Jean Cocteau, a friend of both Proust’s & Laure’s, nicknamed her “Corporal Petrarch.”)
