Bertie, Prince of Wales

Friday August 24, 2018

Although France was not a monarchy at the fin de siècle, crowned heads from all over Europe enjoyed visiting Paris, where the local aristocracy eagerly welcomed them as guests of honor at its social occasions. One of the most beloved & prestigious of the royals who regularly came to Paris was Queen Victoria’s eldest son, Bertie, Prince of Wales (later Edward VII). A connoisseur of the fairer sex, Bertie had love affairs with many noble Parisiennes, including the Princesse de Sagan...despite his close friendship with her husband. Bertie was also a friend of Henry Greffulhe’s--the two of them hunted together often--& admired Elisabeth Greffulhe, though he did not attempt to seduce her. Even so, it was said that Elisabeth’s radiant beauty “made the rain fall & the sun shine for the Prince of Wales.”
