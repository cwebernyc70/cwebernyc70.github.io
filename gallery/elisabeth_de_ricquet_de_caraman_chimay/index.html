Elisabeth de Ricquet de Caraman Chimay

Friday August 17, 2018

Elisabeth de Ricquet de Caraman Chimay, comtesse (initially vicomtesse) Greffulhe (1860-1952)—depicted here at age 20, two years after her marriage to the colossally rich but unloving Henry Greffulhe—was considered one of the foremost beauties of her time. Her every public appearance in Paris caused a stir of excitement as frenzied, wrote one observer, “as if she were visiting from Mount Olympus.” By the time Proust first became fascinated with her in the spring of 1893, when he was 20 and she 32, she had spent 15 years fielding declarations of love and worship from numberless friends and strangers alike. Indeed, her vast private archives, some 200 cartons’ worth of unpublished writings, contain several large boxes dedicated entirely to these "homages and appreciations."
