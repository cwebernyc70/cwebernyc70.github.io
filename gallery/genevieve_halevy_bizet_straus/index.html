Geneviève Halévy Bizet Straus

Friday August 17, 2018

Like Proust, Geneviève Halévy Bizet Straus, photographed here in 1889 by Nadar, belonged to Paris’s bourgeois Jewish elite. Both her father, Fromental Halévy, and her first husband, Georges Bizet, were world-famous composers; while her mother was an heiress from a moneyed Sephardic clan in Bordeaux, and her second husband, lawyer Emile Straus, was rumored to be the illegitimate son of Baron James de Rothschild. Hers was an unusual background for a member of aristocratic society, where titled Catholics predominated, but the grandees of that set were intrigued by Geneviève’s vicarious celebrity and charmed by her vivacious wit. Proust was a schoolmate and friend of her only child, Jacques Bizet, at Paris’s exclusive lycée Condorcet; in 1888-89, Proust’s final year there, Jacques introduced him to Geneviève. She was the first society maven Proust had ever encountered, and it was through her that he would gain his introduction to the lords and ladies of the grand monde.
