Geneviève & Toulmouche

Friday August 24, 2018

As a friend of many Parisian artists, Geneviève Halévy Bizet not only posed for numerous portraits of herself but also served as a model for paintings of unnamed female figures. She particularly enjoyed sitting for her friend Auguste Toulmouche, who cast her as one of the “modern Parisiennes” that peopled such artworks as this painting, The Billet-doux (1883). The profile of the woman depicted here does not match that of Geneviève, whose nose did not have this coquettish upturned tilt. But the deep-set dark eyes, the wavy dark hair, the tawny skin-tone & the pale pink dress--a staple of her “half-mourning” wardrobe since the death of her first husband, Georges Bizet eight years earlier--are all reminiscent of the woman known to her intimates as “the Mauve Muse.”
