Marcel Proust as a Young Man

Friday August 17, 2018

Proust first insinuated himself into Geneviève’s social circle in the year that followed his graduation from Condorcet, while he was doing his compulsory service in the French army. Though he was stationed in Orléans, he spent his Sunday furloughs traveling back to Paris to attend Geneviève’s weekly salon, where she presided over an elite group of noblemen and artists, all of them passionately devoted to her. As Proust admitted to her in a letter, he was embarrassed to have to wear his rather shabby uniform in such refined company, but he did his best to charm the hostess and her friends. Proust’s school friends teased him for kissing up to people who were old enough to be his parents; “Proustifications” was their term for the torrents of fulsome praise he lavished on Geneviève and her other callers.
