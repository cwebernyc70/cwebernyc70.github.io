Faire-part

Friday August 24, 2018

Family connections were (and still are) a defining feature of French aristocratic identity. One means of reinforcing the noble esprit de caste was the faire-part. This document announced a birth, death, engagement or marriage in the family, and it named all the relatives of the individual who had just been born, died, become engaged or gotten married. This one, bordered in black to signify mourning, announced the death in 1893 of Laure de Chevigné’s namesake and cousin the baronne de Raincourt (née Laure de Sade). It was Mme de Raincourt & her husband--members of the Comte de Chambord’s retinue at Frohsdorf, in Austria--who fourteen years earlier had arranged Laure’s marriage to their fellow courtier Comte Adhéaume de Chevigné.
