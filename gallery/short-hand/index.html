Elisabeth's Secret Code

Friday August 24, 2018

Elisabeth Greffulhe wrote compulsively: letters, literary sketches, notes to self, & diary entries. Aware that her husband (whom she & her sisters referred to in their correspondence as “the Jealous Woman” [la Jalouse]) frequently rifled through her papers when she wasn’t around, she developed a secret code for writing about matters she wanted to keep private. She based the code on a mid-19th-century form of stenographers’ shorthand, which she had learned as a girl when acting as her father’s secretary on his diplomatic missions for the King of the Belgians. In this sample of Elisabeth’s coded writing, she describes a titillating scenario in which two young ladies play-fight together in a bathtub, biting each other & pinching each other’s nipples.
