Swans

Friday August 17, 2018

This photo was taken during a fête in the Bois de Boulogne that all three of my book’s heroines attended in 1896—Proust, who was still relatively socially obscure at that point, was not invited. I plan to recount this party in the sequel to Proust’s Duchess, a volume that will trace the trajectories of the three ladies and their self-appointed bard from the end of the nineteenth century to Proust’s death in 1922. I include the image here to underscore the fact that these women were the original “society swans.” As Elaine Showalter remarked in her perceptive review of my book in the New York Times Book Review, they "viewed themselves, and were praised by Proust and others, as the exotic golden birds of French high society. When confined to an aviary of a cage, Elisabeth wrote, woman cultivates her plumage and her song for reasons of pleasure [and] power.’"
