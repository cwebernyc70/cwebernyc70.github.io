Laure de Chevigné

Friday August 17, 2018

Laure de Sade, comtesse Adhéaume de Chevigné (1859-1936), captured here by Nadar in 1887, was recognizable to her contemporaries as the physical prototype for the duchesse de Guermantes, imagined by the narrator of the Search to be “the offspring of a goddess and a swan.” For Proust as for many of her other admirers, Laure’s hooded blue eyes, elegantly beakish nose, strawberry blond hair, and throaty voice, which “seemed to have been roughened by centuries of giving orders,” combined to make her the epitome of noble distinction. In the spring of 1892, Proust stalked her for several weeks in their shared neighborhood on the Right Bank of Paris, trailing behind her at a discreet distance as she carried out her daily round of errands and social calls.
