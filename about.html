About

I am a professor of French & Comparative Literature at Barnard College, Columbia University, where I specialize in 18th & 19th-century French literature, history, & culture. The image on the right is actually a portrait of me painted by my amazingly talented friend Brad Livingstone Black.

My newest book, Proust's Duchess: How Three Celebrated Women Captured the Imagination of Fin-de-Siecle Paris Proust's Duchess (Knopf, 2018), explores the fizzy intersection of aristocracy, celebrity, and artistic genius in fin-de-siecle Paris. The book involved 7 years' worth of detective work in the unpublished private papers of its three heroines, the real-life society doyennes upon whom Marcel Proust modeled his fictional Duchesse de Guermantes, the reigning goddess of the Parisian nobility in In Search of Lost Time.

My previous books are:

  • Queen of Fashion: What Marie Antoinette Wore to the Revolution (2006);
  • Terror and Its Discontents (2003).

I have written extensively for the mainstream press & for fashion magazines as well as for scholarly publications; my aim in all my work is to make my material accessible and engaging to the reader.
