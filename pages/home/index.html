Laure

During the many years I spent working on my latest book, Proust’s Duchess, I amassed several hundred photographs, paintings, and drawings of the people and places I was writing about. Although my publisher generously allowed me to reproduce more than 100 of these pictures in the book, I thought it would be fun to have a place online where interested readers could view both published and unpublished visual material from my research. Below, I’ve provided a few sample images that together form a broad pictorial overview of the story I tell in Proust’s Duchess: the story of the three reigning queens of fin-de-siecle Parisian society who enthralled several generations worth of aristocrats, artists, and writers, including a young Marcel Proust. Transfixed by their breeding, elegance, wit, and style, Proust would go on as a mature author to use them as the models for the composite character of the duchesse de Guermantes, who rules over noble society in his great novel, In Search of Lost Time (1913-1927).
